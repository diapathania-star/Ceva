The Seeker

I was a young child, standing alone on the balcony of my home while my parents were at work, when I first asked myself this question. I could see that there had to be more to life than what I saw around me.

In grade eight, I came across Raja Ram Mohan Roy in my history textbook. For the first time, I felt a surge of inspiration - here was someone who saw what was broken and decided to do something about it. I wanted to be like that. There were a thousand problems in my own home and society. Maybe that was the point of my life.

Instead, I made the practical choice. Studied engineering, got a corporate job with a fancy title and a good paycheque, and built a life in London.

It didn't take long for the question to return. Many sleepless nights later, I quit and came back to India. I needed to find an answer.

The Breaking

A year later, I found it. I discovered my gift - and with it, the purpose of my life.

I chose to walk toward it even without a map. It scared me. I felt lost for a long time. But I also felt more alive than I ever had before, slowly uncovering who I actually was rather than who I had spent years performing.

Then came the hardest stretch. A mental breakdown. Deep loneliness. Most of my business income, gone.

I sat with a question I had never had to face before: who was I beyond the roles I played and the purpose I was chasing?

Facing that question - really facing it, not just moving past it - changed everything. It prepared me to coach from a place I couldn't have reached any other way.

It was in the middle of rebuilding that Ceva came to life. Ceva comes from 'Sewa' - service performed with creativity and joy.

Arjuna realised his potential when he found courage and did the work necessary to walk on the path of a warrior.

He remembered who he was and stepped up to it.
Making the right choice and staying on the path - that is the work we do
