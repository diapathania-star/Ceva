The discipline of wanting less noise

Attention is a leadership practice-start with your own.

APR 14, 2024  ·  4 MIN READ

We live in a world that rewards noise. Busyness. Overcommitting. Saying yes before we've even asked ourselves what matters.

But clarity doesn't arrive in the middle of all that.

The discipline of wanting less noise isn't about escaping life. It's about choosing what deserves your attention-and letting the rest go.

1. Noise feels productive. Clarity feels slow.

Noise gives us the illusion of movement. It fills the space where we might otherwise sit with hard questions.

Clarity, on the other hand, asks for stillness. It asks us to be honest about what we want, what we're avoiding, and what no longer fits.

Clarity is not a rush. It's a return.

2. Attention is a choice.

Where your attention goes, your life goes. Every ping, every scroll, every unnecessary yes is a tiny vote for a life you may not choose.

Start by choosing a few things that truly matter. Protect those spaces.

3. The quiet makes space for what matters.

When the noise recedes, values get louder. Intuition gets clearer. Decisions get easier.

You don't need more time. You need more of you in the time you have.

Less noise isn't about doing less. It's about living more deliberately.

What are you ready to stop saying yes to?
