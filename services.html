Common Questions

Therapy focuses on helping you heal from past incidents, understand your patterns and cope with the mental challenges you’re facing e.g anxiety, depression. It helps you improve your emotional and mental well-being.

Coaching is focussed on the present (where you are right now) and future (how to get to where you want to be). Coaching enables you to find clarity, make decisions, gain confidence and others skills, and grow both personally and professionally.

Leadership coaching enables you to build on your strengths, develop stronger people skills and grow faster whether you are an individual contributor, a new manager, an emerging leader or a senior executive. Your Coach supports you through the professional and people challenges you’re facing at present.

Depends on the challenge or goal you’re working on. There could be a deep discussion on a problem you’re facing in one and discussion on a new concept and its application in another. Sessions are dynamic in nature and are driven by your needs.

However, every session supports you to either learn something new about a problem you are in or discover a blind spot or find inspiration to step outside of your comfort zone or make a decision you were struggling with.

Anyone keen to grow personally, professionally or both benefits from working with a coach because it provides them a space to work deeply and consistently on their chosen area with the support of someone skilled to see their mistakes, blind spots, patterns and more.

More importantly though, a coach helps a person discover the problem underneath the problem, find the right approach for themselves and help them make the most of their strengths and resources. Information found online is broad guidance which helps you move forward but often is unable to help you get to the place you want to be in. Most people who choose to work with a coach don't do it only just because they feel stuck. They recognise what's possible with the right support and are keen to tap into it to get to their destination sooner.

When you hire an architect to design your home, you don’t pay for each visit, you pay for their ability to understand what you want and help create it. And the home they design lasts you for a long time, sometimes your whole life.

Coaching works similarly for us. We don't think in terms of sessions. We are interested in where you want to get to and the time, attention and care it would genuinely take to help you get there. That's what the pricing of working with us reflects. The price for coaching varies from coach to coach, depending on their expertise and approach. There's no fixed number across the industry. You can have an honest conversation with them about what feels right for both of you.

This is driven by your unique challenges and goals.

If you come with a short-term goal, it can take anywhere between a month to six months. A leap in your career growth and/or leadership development related work requires at least 12 to 18 months.

Passionate & committed individuals, though, are always working with a coach because they find in it a space to speak without filter or judgment, reflect bravely, find the right direction, make important decisions and keep up with life’s challenges. People also get coached to create more peace, fulfilment and happiness in their personal lives, which are again long-term goals.

A coach creates a space for you in which you feel understood, seen, supported and loved for who you are. Thus, the way you feel in the presence of a coach is an important criteria to consider while deciding on the right one for yourself. In addition to this, speaking to them about your challenges, learning about their approach and experience also helps.

The right coach for you is like a life partner, they look different for everyone but with the right coach you experience chemistry, love, support and a deep understanding of you and your journey.

What happens when you don’t solve that problem in the next few months, what would suffer in that case? How long have you been trying to do it by yourself?

And what areas of your life are being affected by your challenges or lack of progress (in other words what is the opportunity cost you’re paying for it)? The answers to these questions can help you make the right decision for yourself.
